Before
Area 51's site needed a total redesign, not only was their site visually unpleasing but it also wasn't easy for consumers to navigate. The site also did not allow for customers to purchase the products online.
Persona
Through a series of interviews with Area 51 shoppers (as well as potential Area 51 shoppers) a persona was established that clearly outlined the goals and needs of their furniture shopping experience.
Research
In order to sell to furniture shoppers I needed to know what they look for when they shop. I discovered that many millienials prefer their furniture to be one of a kind or vintage. With a large number of Area 51's merchandise being reclaimed vintage pieces, this appeared to be an area for improvement. If Area 51 focused more of their traffic on vintage items they could draw in more customers that are seeking out unique pieces.
  • 63% of millennials age 35 and under prefer vintage
  • 1 in 4 want one of a kind pieces
  • Second hand accounts for 4% of sales, this amounts to about 4 billion in retail sales
The Solution
Create a website for Area 51 that caters specifically to the goals and needs of the established persona
Focus the customer's attention on vintage/reclaimed goods
Quick and easy shopping experience with a simple checkout procedure
Rebranding
Area 51's old site was in need of rebranding. The site did not appeal to the younger crowd that was drawn to the Mid Century Modern inventory. Below is a mock up of a potential ad that focuses the customer's attention on the vintage goods that are in high demand by the established persona.
Paper Prototyping
I sketched based on the flow a user would typically take when shopping, from discovery of item to the final payment. I created rough sketches and then moved on to a workable paper-proto that I used to test on several users. The results of the test helped me organize the product details page. Many users were overlooking important information about the pieces of furniture because it was hidden under a plus sign.
User Testing and Iterations
I began my low fidelity wireframes by applying what I learned from my paper-proto tests. Users wanted all of the product details right in front so they don't have to hunt for it. I also found that during the checkout process users did not like the stages of checkout to be collapsable (as shown in the paper-proto).
