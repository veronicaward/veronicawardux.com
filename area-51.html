Area 51

The Challenge

  • Redesign a dated Mid-Century Modern furniture store's website
  • Use the store's current cutsomer base to gather qualitative data
  • Establish a persona
  • Design the new site layout for the established persona
  • Create a clickable prototype

    • Client

    Area 51

    Platform

    Desktop

    Software Used

    Sketch, Invision

    Research

    I started this project by reading about the furniture shopping habits of modern day consumers. In order to sell to furniture shoppers I needed to know what they look for when they shop. I discovered that many millienials prefer their furniture to be one of a kind or vintage. With a large number of Area 51's merchandise being reclaimed vintage pieces, this appeared to be an area for improvement. If Area 51 focused more of their traffic on vintage items it could draw in more customers that are seeking out original pieces.

    Persona
    • 47 Screener surveys
    • 7 User interviews
    The Solution
    • Create a website for Area 51 that caters specifically to the needs of the established persona
    • Provide detailed, up-to-date information about the inventory within the store
    • Focus the customer's attention on vintage/reclaimed goods
    Paper Prototyping

    I sketched the stages of checkout, from discovery of item to the final payment. I started with very rough sketches and then moved on to a workable paper-proto that I used to test on several users. The results of the test helped me organize the product details page. I found many users weren't noticing a lot of information about the pieces of furniture because it was hidden under a plus sign.

    User Testing and Iterations

    I began my low fidelity wireframes by applying what I learned from my paper-proto tests. I found that users wanted all of the product details right in front so they don't have to hunt for it. I also found that during the checkout process users did not like the stages of checkout to be collapsable (as shown in the paper-proto). They seemed to be able to navigate the process much easier when a progress bar was visible instead.

    Final Iteration
    • Stay up to date on inventory
    • Browse all vintage products at once
    • Quick and easy checkout process
