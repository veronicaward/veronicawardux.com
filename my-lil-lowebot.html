My Lil' Lowebot

The Challenge

  • Create an app that educates customers on how to interact with the new in-store robot (Lowebot)
  • Encourage customer interaction with Lowebot
  • Free up sales associates time by diverting questions to Lowebot
  • Help customers understand all of the ways that Lowebot can improve their shopping experience

Client

Fellow Robots and Lowe's

Platform

Nexus 5

Software Used

Axure, Photoshop

Role

UX Designer, Design Lead, Interviewer

Background
Lowe's partnered with Fellow Robots to develop a customer service robot that helps shoppers navigate the store. The robot guides the customer to the location of the item and if the shopper has a specific question about a project or tool they can be connected to a service professional through the screen that is located on the front of the robot. Lowe's wants to free up employees' time to focus on more one on one customer service. Lowebot is there to help customers as well as employees of Lowe's.
Research
In order to become more familiar with Lowebot and how customers are currently reacting to the bot, we called the current store that is housing Lowebot. We quickly found out that many customers were not engaging Lowebot as much as they hoped. Our research uncovered that the number one question being asked to Lowebot was "Where is the bathroom?". Customers weren't taking advantage of the amazing capabilities of Lowebot, nor were they even aware of what Lowebot could even do. We concluded that in order to educate customers on what Lowebot can do we needed to create an app that made Lowebot fun and easy to understand.
Persona
The Solution
  • Summon Lowebot to you from any location within the Lowe's store
  • Educate shoppers on how Lowebot can help them
  • Make interacting with Lowebot fun to the customer by creating voice and speed controls
Paper Prototype
Once we knew what the persona needed we started sketching rough drafts of the prototype and began testing. After testing we found that there were so many features and screens that users were getting confused and lost within the app. With the whole purupse of the app being to simplify things with Lowebot, we realized we needed to scale back and make the process much less complex.
User Testing and Iterations
Upon testing our high fidelity mock up we discovered that users weren't reading through the FAQ page. Another large problem was that many users couldn't identify what the "summon" button did. The icon wasn't distinct enough for a large number of people to instantly identify. Many users were also alarmed at the idea of accidently hitting the "summon" button and sending the robot unintentionally.
Final Iteration
In our final iteration we chose to include a video of a customer interacting with the robot, a short informative tutorial of what the app can do. Supplying the video as well as the text helped users figure out what their next steps were once entering the app. We chose to replace the summon button with a map icon on the FAQ page. The map icon would then take you to the store map with the location of you and Lowebot. Within this page you can then summon Lowebot by hitting the "Summon Lowebot" button.
Success Metrics
  • Track the increase or decrease of customer in-store foot traffic
  • Track how often customers come back to continue to use Lowebot
  • Track the number of views of the tutorial video
  • Track the amount of time focused on the "frequently asked questions" page; are customers reading through?
