The Challenge

  • Redesign a dated Mid-Century Modern furniture store's website
  • Use the store's current cutsomer base to gather qualitative data
  • Establish a persona
  • Design the new site layout for the established persona
  • Create a clickable prototype

    • Client

    Area 51

    Platform

    Desktop

    Software Used

    Sketch, Invision

    Research

    I first began this project by researching the top furniture store websites (and the best Mid-Century Modern furniture stores). I read about the furniture shopping habits of customers today and discovered that many millienials prefer their furniture to be one of a kind or vintage. With a large number of Area 51's merchandise being reclaimed vintage pieces, this appeared to be a large miss.

    Persona

  • 47 Screener surveys
  • 7 User Interviews

    • The Solution
    • Create a website for Area 51 that caters specifically to the needs of the established persona
    • Provide detailed, up-to-date information about the inventory within the store
    • Focus the customer's attention on vintage/reclaimed goods
    Paper Prototyping

    I sketched the stages of checkout, from discovery of item to the final payment. I started with very rough sketches and then moved on to a workable paper-proto that I used to test on several users. The results of the test helped me organize the product details page. I found many users weren't noticing a lot of information about the pieces of furniture because it was hidden under a plus sign.

    User Testing and Iterations

    I began my low fidelity wireframe by applying what I learned from my paper-proto tests. I found that users wanted all of the product details right infront so they don't feel like they have to hunt for it. I also found that during the checkout process users did not like the stages of checkout to be collapseable (like in the paper-proto). They seemed to be able to navigate the process much easier when a progress bar was visable instead.

    Final Iteration
