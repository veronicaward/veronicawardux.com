Quiz Quarrel

The Challenge

  • Create custom quizzes with ease
  • Create a quiz filled embaressing content only your friends would understand
  • Privacy settings for all quizzes to make sure your quizzes are only seen by who you choose
  • Have fun making quizzes filled with jokes
  • Make quizzes easy to find and play
  • Challenge your friends in head to head to challenges

Client

Quiz Quarrel

Platform

Desktop

Software Used

Illustrator, Photoshop

Role

UX / UI Designer, Researcher, User Tester

Background
In the summer of 2015 my friend good Josh decided to leave Seattle to travel across the United States with his boyfriend. They wanted to explore small town Americana. I was thrilled to see my friend moving on to bigger and better things, but I was going to miss him. For my 30th birthday my boyfriend Alan, (who is a software engineer) decided to make me a gift that helped me connect with my friend from a distance. Alan made it possible for Josh to log in and make a private quiz just for me to see that included all of our ridiculous inside jokes.
The Problem
I was thrilled to play these games as a birthday present, so much so that I wanted to be able create quizzes for Josh too. The problem was that Alan didn't make it possible to create picture quizzes (the kind of quiz I wanted to make). Also, what he did have set up was far from user friendly, because it was only meant to make a few quizzes for one person. We decided it would be a fun project to make a fully functional quiz site that would let users create private quizzes just for a few people to see (as well as some that everyone can see).
The Product

 

 

What sets Quiz Quarrel apart from other quiz sites is its ability to create a wider variety of quiz types. Users have the choice to pick from a larger selection of game layouts.

 

 

Users also have the unique feature of being able to challenge their friends to a duel. This means you and your friends can go head to head in a quiz of your choosing.
Style Guide and Redlines
Developers need very specific directions in order to execute the vision you have mocked up. In order to effectivly communicate the design style to my developer I created redlines and a style guide for him to refer to.
