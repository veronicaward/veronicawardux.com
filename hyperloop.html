Hyperloop

A new highspeed form of transportation
The Challenge
  • Design a ticket purchasing interface that will travel to 4 cities that is specifically catered to the provided persona
  • Each pod must seat 100 people
  • Evaluate the ticket buying process to simplify as much as possible
  • Research communting habits
  • Create a clickable prototype

Client

Hyperloop

Platform

Mobile

Software Used

Photoshop, Illustrator, Sketch

Role

UX Designer, UI Designer

Invision Prototype

View Prototype
Research
  • Researched most annoying habits of commuters to find out what would make the experience the most enjoyable
    • - Hates the smell of other peoples food and hearing them chew loudly

  • Researched commuters mental models of the new form of transportation
    • - Of the people interviewed, user's mental models matched a train
    - Users don't view the hyperloop to be similar to the subway

  • Researched ticket buying process of different forms of fransporation
    • - Performed a competitive analysis of Amtrak and Thalys
    - Performed a task analysis of how to buy a ticket for a subway, train and airplane

Persona

The persona provided:

- A traveling tech savy business person in their 40s
Competitive Analysis
  • User reviews of Thalys complained that they had to type in their card number every time they buy a ticket
  • A large number of complaints about the UI for Thalys and Amtrak
Paper Prototyping
After researching the preferences of commuters, analyzing the ticket purchasing process, and analyzing competitive apps, I began to sketch the flow a user would take to purchase tickets with the new hyperloop app. I sketched screen states that filled the requirements of the tasks needed to get tickets.
User Testing and Iterations
After feeling comfortable enough with the sketches I made I brought the prototype to life using Sketch and InVision and tested with users who fit the persona that was provided to me. I discovered that users were clicking the box of the city where they were currently instead of the intention which was their desired destination. I also discovered users were feeling anxious about the purchase phase, they wanted to be reminded of how much they were going to be charged.
