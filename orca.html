ORCA

The Challenge

  • Make getting a new ORCA card understandable for all users
  • Allow users to know of the different paths they can take to obtain a card
  • Educate users on the reduced fare options available

Client

ORCA

Platform

Desktop

Software Used

Illustrator, Photoshop

Role

UX / UI Designer, Researcher, User Tester

Background

Last year, Seattle grew faster than any other major American city, according to population estimates released by the Census Bureau. The main reason for Seattle's rapid growth is the booming tech industry, the more the industry grows the more the city attracts out of towners.

All of these out of towners have one thing in common, they all want to take advantage of the great public transportation system in Seattle. I am originally from Detroit where public transportation is almost non-existent, when I moved to Seattle I did not know where to start when it came to getting a bus pass. I went on the ORCA "get a card" page to learn how to obtain a card and found myself more confused than when I started.

The Problem
Getting an ORCA card should be easy, especially in a city that is growing at such a rapid pace. The site available to the public now makes the process of getting a card intimidating and confusing.
Analysis
To catagorize the information provided on the current ORCA site I had to perform an analysis of the page. The analysis assisted in simplifing the data provided on the site, it helped to outlined the different ways someone can get an ORCA card.
User Interviews
Performing user interviews allowed me the opportunity to understand what information was most valuable to the end user. It also helped guide the structure of information, this was critical to prioritize what information is seen first.
The Product
Success Metrics
  • Track if there are reduced calls to customer care
  • Track the increase or decrease in the number of cards issued
